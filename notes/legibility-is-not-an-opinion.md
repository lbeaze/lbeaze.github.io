---
title: Legibility is Not an Opinion
date: 2026-04-07
tags: accessibility, design, craft
---

There's a category of design feedback I've learned to push back on immediately: "It feels a little heavy" or "Can we lighten it up?" when someone is talking about text. Because legibility isn't a matter of taste — it's a matter of physics and human biology.

The eye needs a certain luminance ratio between text and its background to resolve letterforms clearly. WCAG's 4.5:1 minimum for normal text isn't arbitrary. It's derived from research on contrast sensitivity across the population — including people with moderately low vision, people in bright environments, and people on screens with poor calibration. Meeting that number means your text is readable for a much wider range of people in a much wider range of conditions.

When a stakeholder asks to "lighten it up," what they're often describing is an aesthetic preference formed by looking at a well-calibrated monitor in a dim room. That's not a valid sample of real-world conditions. Push back with data, not opinion — and then offer them something that's both lighter *and* legible, because that solution almost always exists.
